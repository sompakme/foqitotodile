You Wanna Move to Georgia? We Got Cities...Lots of 'Em!

So, you've got your sweet tea brewing and your peaches picked fresh, because you're thinking about setting up shop in Georgia, huh? Well, good choice, my friend! But before you start picturing yourself on a rocking chair on a porch swing overlooking a quaint little town (although, we do have those too), you might be wondering: just how many cities does Georgia have anyway?

Buckle Up, Buttercup, It's a Numberama!

Alright, alright, settle down now. We know you came here for some facts, and facts you shall receive! Here's the thing about Georgia: we like our local governments. Like, a lot. That means there are a bunch of different ways a place can be incorporated, from cities to towns to consolidated city-counties (don't worry, we'll get to what that means in a sec).

Here's the grand total, courtesy of the folks over at the U.S. Census Bureau: a whopping 535 municipalities! That's a lot of places to unpack your cowboy boots (or your flip flops, no judgement here).

Now, Hold on a Sec, What About Consolidated City-County Doohickeys?

Ah, yes, the consolidated city-county. Basically, it's when a city and its surrounding county decide to join forces and become one big, happy governmental family. Think of it as a marriage between Batman and Robin – they're both awesome on their own, but together they're unstoppable! (Except, you know, for crime and stuff, but you get the idea.)

There are eight of these consolidated city-county things in Georgia, so you might see them on a map and wonder, "Wait, is this a city or a county?" The answer is: yes. It's both!

So, How Many "Regular" Cities Does Georgia Have?

Alright, alright, with all this talk of consolidation, you just want a simple number, right? We hear you. If you're sticking to just the traditional cities and towns (minus those merged with counties), then you're looking at a number somewhere in the ballpark of around 527.

But hey, who's counting? The important thing is, there's a place for everyone in Georgia, from the bustling metropolis of Atlanta to the charming small towns where everyone knows your name.

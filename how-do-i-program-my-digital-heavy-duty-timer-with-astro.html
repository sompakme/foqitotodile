What is a digital timer, and how does it work?

Digital timers are used to keep track of the passage of time; they may be used to trigger an action, to start timing once an action is initiated, or to do both. Some goods can be programmed, however others may be fixed at a specific internal time and function, depending on the manufacturer.

What is the operation of a digital timer?

It is the electrical timer circuit that keeps track of how many pulses have been generated by the oscillator and triggers particular actions when a specified number of pulses have been generated. Another component of the circuit may then count the seconds until a minute has elapsed, after which it would increase the minute counter by one. Timers may also communicate with other devices by sending signals to them.

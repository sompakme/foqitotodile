Last Chance U, the hit Netflix documentary series, has captivated audiences with its raw and emotional look into the lives of junior college football players who are on their last chance to fulfill their Division I football dreams. Season 2, which premiered in 2017, followed the East Mississippi Community College (EMCC) Lions for another season of highs and lows. As the players strive for redemption and success on the field, their off-field journeys are equally compelling. Fans often wonder where these athletes are now and how their lives have unfolded since the cameras stopped rolling. This article delves into the whereabouts of the key figures from Last Chance U Season 2.

Introduction to Last Chance U Season 2

Last Chance U’s second season continued to document the trials and tribulations of the football players at EMCC. The series provided an in-depth look at the challenges faced by these young men, many of whom dealt with academic struggles, personal issues, and the immense pressure to perform athletically. As we revisit the cast, it’s important to see how their experiences at EMCC shaped their futures.

De’Andre Johnson: From EMCC to FAU and Beyond

De’Andre Johnson’s journey was one of the central storylines of Season 2. After his dismissal from Florida State University, Johnson sought redemption at EMCC. Following his time at the junior college, he transferred to Florida Atlantic University (FAU) to play under coach Lane Kiffin. Johnson’s time at FAU was marked by competition and injuries, but he managed to earn his degree. As of now, Johnson has had stints in the Canadian Football League (CFL) and continues to pursue a professional football career.

Isaiah Wright’s Turbulent Path

Isaiah Wright’s talent on the field was undeniable, but his life took a dramatic turn after EMCC. Wright faced legal issues that were widely publicized, casting uncertainty over his future. After dealing with the legal system, Wright has worked to rebuild his life and has expressed a desire to return to football, though his current status in the sport remains unclear.

Chauncey Rivers: From EMCC to the NFL

Chauncey Rivers was a standout defensive player at EMCC who later transferred to Georgia State University. His performance there led him to be signed as an undrafted free agent by the Baltimore Ravens in the NFL. Rivers’ journey from junior college to the professional league is a testament to his talent and determination.

Tim Bonner’s Continued Football Pursuits

Tim Bonner’s personality made him a fan favorite, and his football journey did not end at EMCC. He transferred to Florida Atlantic University alongside De’Andre Johnson. After his collegiate career, Bonner has explored opportunities in professional football, including trials with the NFL and other leagues.

Kam Carter’s Path After EMCC

Kam Carter’s time at EMCC was a stepping stone to further his football career. He transferred to the University of Pittsburgh, where he played for the Panthers. Post-college, Carter has looked to find a place in professional football, with aspirations to join the NFL or other professional leagues.

Dakota Allen’s Success Story

Dakota Allen’s redemption arc was one of the most inspiring of the series. After EMCC, he returned to Texas Tech University, the school from which he was initially dismissed. Allen became a team captain and was later drafted by the Los Angeles Rams in the 2019 NFL Draft. He has since spent time with various NFL teams, showcasing the resilience and talent that were evident during his time at EMCC.

Other Notable Players and Where They Are Now

Beyond the main cast, several other players have continued their football careers or have moved on to different paths:

  • John Franklin III (Wide Receiver/QB) – Transitioned to a wide receiver and had stints with NFL teams, including the Chicago Bears.
  • Chauncey Rivers (Defensive Lineman) – As mentioned, he made it to the NFL with the Baltimore Ravens.
  • Calvin Jackson Jr. (Wide Receiver) – Transferred to Washington State University and has pursued a professional career.

Coaching Staff and Their Careers

The coaching staff at EMCC also played pivotal roles in the series. Head coach Buddy Stephens and academic advisor Brittany Wagner became household names. Stephens continues to lead the EMCC football program, while Wagner has left the college and now runs her own academic consulting business.

Impact of Last Chance U on Players’ Lives

The exposure from Last Chance U has had varying impacts on the players’ lives. For some, it has opened doors to new opportunities, while for others, it has brought challenges, especially when dealing with newfound fame and public scrutiny.

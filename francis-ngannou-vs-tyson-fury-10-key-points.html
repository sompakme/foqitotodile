Francis Ngannou vs. Tyson Fury: 10 Key Points

August 11, 2024
blog

In a thrilling bout in Riyadh, Tyson Fury emerged battered but victorious in a contentious split-decision win over the extraordinary Francis Ngannou, who was making his boxing debut. One judge scored the fight 95-94 in favor of Francis Ngannou, while another had the same score for Fury. The third official’s scorecard raised eyebrows with a 96-93 margin for the WBC world champion.

This 10-round non-title fight saw Ngannou challenge the champion with unyielding determination and fire, denting Fury‘s prestige. Fury suffered significant swelling around his left eye, casting doubt on his highly anticipated fight for the undisputed world heavyweight title against Oleksandr Usyk, scheduled for December 23.

Usyk, the holder of the IBF, WBA, and WBO titles, entered the ring to express his readiness to face Fury on the agreed date. While Fury initially seemed eager, his promoter, Frank Warren, acknowledged the need for careful consideration, as the quick turnaround offers limited time for recovery from his intense clash with Ngannou.

Tyson Fury vs. Francis Ngannou: The Clash of Titans

1. Francis Ngannou’s Unexpected Tenacity

Predictions in the world of boxing and Fury’s past fights all pointed to Francis struggling if the bout lasted beyond round three. We underestimated you, Mr. Ngannou, and for that, we apologize.

We overlooked your incredible journey from a poverty-stricken upbringing in Cameroon, surviving on discarded food, laboring at the age of ten, enduring multiple arrests and deportations, and sleeping on the streets of Paris. Your path to becoming the UFC heavyweight champion through sheer determination, relentless grit, and an iron fist is something we should never forget.

2. The Charisma of Tyson Fury

It’s hard not to admire Tyson Fury. His charismatic personality and sportsmanship shine brightly. He elevated his opponent for this fight, acknowledging the importance of taking it seriously to avoid a blemish on his record. This elevates Francis‘ performance, as Fury’s seriousness adds an extra layer of challenge to the exhibition match.

3. The Power of Determination

By round four, Francis was visibly fatigued, but he never retreated; instead, he relentlessly pursued Fury, searching for an opportunity to unleash his devastating power punches. While lacking in boxing finesse, he compensated with heart and even displayed moments of skill, switching stances and expertly evading Fury‘s knockdown attempts.

4. The Game-Changing Knockdown

The perfectly executed knockdown was a moment of sheer beauty. It altered the fight’s course, as Fury was wary of Ngannou‘s formidable power. This prompted Fury to maintain distance, relying on jabs and nimble footwork to avoid Ngannou‘s devastating right hand.

5. The Frustration with Anthony Joshua and Deontay Wilder

This fight highlighted the shortcomings of other heavyweight boxers. It’s baffling that Anthony Joshua, with over 20 professional fights, still struggles with basic movement, while Ngannou, at 37 and seemingly less agile, displayed superior defensive skills. As for Deontay Wilder, his performance further emphasized his limitations, underscoring the current state of the heavyweight division.

6. Francis Ngannou’s Triumph Regardless of the Result

Ngannou‘s journey from obscurity to this level of competition is a remarkable victory. He faced adversity, including conflicts with Dana White and MMA fans who accused him of greed, and still earned respect and admiration while maintaining his integrity.

7. The Supportive Trio

The camaraderie and support between Francis Ngannou, Israel Adesanya, and Kamaru Usman is heartwarming. These former UFC champions, who ruled their respective divisions with unmatched skills and ferocity, took their dethronement in stride, understanding the impermanence of success.

8. The Implications of Victory

If Francis had won, it would have been a blow to heavyweight boxing and boxing. MMA enthusiasts would have boasted about the MMA heavyweight champion outperforming a seasoned boxer. Dana White would have been in an awkward position with Francis promoting MMA positively outside the cage.
